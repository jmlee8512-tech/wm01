Fast Fashion

패스트 패션이란?

The fashion industry is one of the world's biggest polluters. Fast fashion encourages people to buy cheap, trendy clothes and throw them away quickly. Every year, the fashion industry produces 92 million tons of textile waste and uses enormous amounts of water and energy.

Microplastics

보이지 않는 오염

About 60% of clothing is made from synthetic fibers like polyester and nylon. Every time we wash these clothes, thousands of tiny plastic particles — called microplastics — are released into waterways. These particles harm marine life and eventually enter our food chain.

A Global Crisis

세계로 퍼지는 문제

Chile's Atacama Desert has become a massive dump for discarded clothing shipped from around the world. Developing countries in Latin America and Africa receive thousands of tons of unwanted clothes each year. Many end up buried in landfills or burned, creating serious health and environmental crises.

Sustainable Solutions

우리가 할 수 있는 것

We can all make a difference: buy fewer but higher-quality clothes, choose secondhand items, repair clothing instead of replacing it, and recycle old textiles. By making smarter choices, each of us can help reduce the fashion industry's impact on our planet.
